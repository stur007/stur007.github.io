My name is **Yutong Ren (任宇桐)**. I am an undergraduate student in physics at [Peking University](https://www.pku.edu.cn/), originally from Taiyuan, Shanxi. My main interests lie in **high-energy physics**, **mathematical physics**, and the use of **machine learning** in scientific research.

## Research

I am currently working as a research student in the PKU CMS group, where I am learning about machine-learning methods for searches for new resonant states of the Higgs boson. Much of my day-to-day work involves reading papers, studying the underlying physics, and building analysis or visualization workflows with **Python** and **ROOT**.

I am still at an early stage, so this website is also a record of that process: what I read, what I build, and what I am slowly beginning to understand more clearly.

## This Website

This site is not meant to be a full online resume. It is closer to a public notebook.

Here I usually write about:

- reading notes in particle physics and mathematics
- small coding projects and experiments
- occasional reflections on books, films, and university life

I like the idea that a personal website should show not only what someone has done, but also what they are genuinely thinking about.

## Beyond Physics

Outside coursework and research, I also spend time reading literature, watching films, and writing down thoughts that feel worth returning to later. That is why this blog mixes technical notes with more personal essays. I prefer that balance: serious study on one side, curiosity about the wider world on the other.
